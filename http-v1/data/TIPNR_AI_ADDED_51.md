For each entry: **SOURCE** = what Strong's + TIPNR already had. **ADDED** = what the AI enrichment added (meaning + significance).

## G0013 Agabus
- **SOURCE (Strong's):** of Hebrew origin (compare H02285) Agabus, an Israelite  
- **SOURCE (TIPNR):** Man living at the time of the New Testament  
- **ADDED meaning:** From Hebrew or Aramaic; possibly "locust" or "loved." Agabus = prophet from Jerusalem (Acts 11:28, 21:10).  
- **ADDED significance:** Prophesied famine (Acts 11:28) and Paul's binding (Acts 21:10–11). Name less significant than function: prophet who speaks the Lord's word to the church.

## G0067 Agrippa
- **SOURCE (Strong's):** apparently from G66 (ἄγριος) and G2462 (ἵππος) wild-horse tamer; Agrippas, one of the Herods  
- **SOURCE (TIPNR):** A Tetrarch | Tetrarch living at the time of the New Testament  
- **ADDED meaning:** Greek Ἀγρίππας (Agrippas); Latin Agrippa, from "born with difficulty." Herod Agrippa II (Acts 25–26).  
- **ADDED significance:** Hears Paul's defense; "almost persuaded" (Acts 26:28). Judge who recognizes Paul's innocence but does not free him. Name (difficult birth) and his dynasty's troubled relation to the truth.
