# ROCK — Word Study

## Symbolic Meaning

| Form | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **One Word** | Covenant |
| **Two Words** | Unchanging Covenant |
| **Sentence** | The binding, unchanging promise/agreement between God and man — the everlasting covenant that never fails |

**Opposite**: SAND = Broken promises / Changing agreements / Unreliable word

---

## The Argument

### 1. Christ IS the Rock

> **1 Corinthians 10:4** — "they drank of that spiritual Rock that followed them: and **that Rock was Christ**"

This explicit identification is the key starting point.

### 2. Christ IS the Mediator of the Covenant

> **Hebrews 8:6** — "he is the **mediator** of a better **covenant**"

> **Hebrews 12:24** — "Jesus the **mediator** of the **new covenant**"

> **Hebrews 13:20** — "the blood of the **everlasting covenant**"

If ROCK = Christ, and Christ = Mediator of the Covenant, then ROCK → COVENANT.

### 3. The Symbolic Chain

The path to the symbolic meaning:

```
ROCK (physical)
  ↓ What characterizes a rock?
UNCHANGING (doesn't shift like sand)
  ↓ What is unchanging between God and man?
COVENANT (everlasting promise)
```

### 4. God's Covenants Are "Everlasting" (Unchanging)

| Reference | Phrase |
|-----------|--------|
| Genesis 9:16 | "everlasting covenant" (Noah) |
| Genesis 17:7, 13, 19 | "everlasting covenant" (Abraham) |
| Ezekiel 16:60 | "I will establish unto thee an everlasting covenant" |
| Ezekiel 37:26 | "everlasting covenant... for evermore" |
| Hebrews 13:20 | "the blood of the everlasting covenant" |

The covenant is the UNCHANGING thing — it doesn't shift, it doesn't fail.

### 5. Deuteronomy 32:4 — The Rock's Character

> "He is the **Rock**, his work is perfect: for all his ways are judgment: a God of **truth** (H530 emunah = faithfulness) and without iniquity, just and right is he."

The Rock is characterized by:
- Perfect work
- Faithfulness (covenant-keeping)
- Justice and righteousness

This is the character of a covenant-keeper.

### 6. The Rock-Sand Contrast (Matthew 7:24-27)

| ROCK | SAND |
|------|------|
| House stands when storms come | House falls |
| Unchanging foundation | Shifting foundation |
| **Covenant** (binding, reliable) | **Broken promises** (unreliable) |

Building on the ROCK = Building on the COVENANT (God's unchanging promise)
Building on SAND = Building on shifting, unreliable foundations

## Methodological Note: The Symbolic Chain

This symbol required **following the chain deeper**:

1. **First attempt**: "Foundation" — too literal (physical synonym)
2. **Second attempt**: "Faithful" — undefined concept
3. **Defined FAITH first** (Obedience) — dependency resolved
4. **Re-evaluated**: "Unchanging" — better, but still abstract
5. **Asked**: "What does UNCHANGING symbolize?"
6. **Found**: Unchanging → Everlasting → COVENANT

**Lesson**: Don't stop at the first abstract concept. Keep asking "what does THIS symbolize?" until you reach a concrete biblical concept.

---

## Conclusion

**ROCK** symbolically represents **COVENANT** — the unchanging, everlasting, binding agreement between God and man. Christ IS the Rock because He IS the mediator of the new/everlasting covenant.

This is why:
- We BUILD on the Rock (our life is built on God's covenant promises)
- We STAND on the Rock (the covenant gives us stability)
- The Rock doesn't MOVE (covenants are binding and unchanging)
- SAND is the opposite (shifting, unreliable, broken promises)
