# 7 — Number Study

## Symbolic Meaning

| Form | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **One Word** | Perfection |
| **Two Words** | Spiritual Completeness |
| **Sentence** | The number of divine perfection and spiritual completeness—representing the fullness of God's work, rest after labor, and the totality of His purposes accomplished |

**Opposite**: Incomplete / Imperfect / Unfinished

---

## The Argument

### 1. The Seventh Day: Divine Rest

> **Genesis 2:2-3** — "And on the **seventh day** God ended his work which he had made; and he **rested** on the seventh day from all his work... God blessed the **seventh day**, and sanctified it"

God's work was COMPLETE on the seventh day. Seven marks the end of labor and the beginning of rest—perfection achieved.

### 2. Seven in Revelation: Complete Patterns

| Sevens in Revelation | Reference |
|---------------------|-----------|
| Seven churches | Rev 1:4, 11 |
| Seven spirits | Rev 1:4; 4:5 |
| Seven golden candlesticks | Rev 1:12 |
| Seven stars | Rev 1:16 |
| Seven seals | Rev 5:1 |
| Seven trumpets | Rev 8:2 |
| Seven bowls/vials | Rev 16:1 |
| Seven thunders | Rev 10:3-4 |

Revelation is structured around SEVENS—God's complete and perfect judgment and redemption.

### 3. Seven Feasts of Israel

> **Leviticus 23** — The seven appointed feasts:

1. Passover
2. Unleavened Bread
3. Firstfruits
4. Pentecost (Weeks)
5. Trumpets
6. Day of Atonement
7. Tabernacles

SEVEN feasts marking God's complete redemptive calendar.

### 4. Jericho: Seven Days, Seven Times

> **Joshua 6:4** — "**seven** priests shall bear before the ark **seven** trumpets of rams' horns: and the **seventh** day ye shall compass the city **seven** times"

Complete victory required SEVEN priests, SEVEN trumpets, and SEVEN circuits on the SEVENTH day.

### 5. Seven Spirits of God

> **Isaiah 11:2** — "the Spirit of the LORD shall rest upon him, the spirit of **wisdom** and **understanding**, the spirit of **counsel** and **might**, the spirit of **knowledge** and of the **fear of the LORD**"

The Spirit's complete manifestation in SEVEN aspects (one Spirit with six attributes = 7).

### 6. Seventy Times Seven: Complete Forgiveness

> **Matthew 18:21-22** — "Lord, how oft shall my brother sin against me, and I forgive him? till seven times? Jesus saith unto him, I say not unto thee, Until seven times: but, Until **seventy times seven**"

Complete, unlimited forgiveness = 70 × 7 = 490.

### 7. The Mathematical Significance

Seven = Three (divine) + Four (earth/creation)

When the divine (3) combines with the earthly (4), the result is spiritual perfection (7)—God's purposes fully accomplished in creation.

## Seven as Covenant Marker

The Hebrew word for seven (שֶׁבַע / sheva) is related to the word for oath/covenant (שָׁבַע / shaba). To "seven" oneself is to bind oneself completely.

> **Genesis 21:28-31** — Abraham gave **seven** ewe lambs... "because I have **sworn** (shaba)"

## Summary

**SEVEN** symbolizes **SPIRITUAL PERFECTION**—the completeness of God's work and rest. It appears throughout Scripture in structures of seven (days, feasts, churches, seals, trumpets, bowls) to signal that God's purposes are fully accomplished. Seven is the sum of divine (3) and earthly (4), representing heaven and earth united in perfection.
