## Recommended Studies

- **Fall of Babylon the Great** — Jesus's list of signs (wars, rumors, nation-against-nation, famine, pestilence, upheaval) comes directly from Jeremiah 51:46 and 2 Chronicles 15:5-6, where it describes what happens when a civilization replaces God's authority with its own. That system has a biblical name — Babylon — and Jesus is telling his disciples they're watching its collapse mechanism activate.
- **Earthquake (Seismos)** — Jesus didn't say "earthquakes." The Greek word seismos means any violent commotion — the KJV translates it "tempest" six chapters earlier (Mat 8:24). When He says "seismoi in various places," He means political, economic, and social upheavals shaking every established order — not just the ground.
- **Birth Pains** — Jesus calls these signs "the beginning of birth pains," not "the beginning of the end." Birth pains are a mechanism that always produces something new — they precede deliverance, not just destruction. This reframes the whole passage from a doomsday forecast into a survival briefing for people living through a transition.
