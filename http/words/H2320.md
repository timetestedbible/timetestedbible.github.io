## Calendar Implications

### The Creation Mandate (Genesis 1:14-16)

> "God made the two **great lights**... the lesser light to govern the night **with the stars**."

For the moon to be a "great light" governing the night "with the stars":
- **Full moon:** Visible dusk to dawn, co-ruling the entire night with visible constellations
- **Crescent moon:** Vanishes quickly after sunset; cannot co-rule with stars
- **Dark moon:** Provides no light at all

### Predictability (1 Samuel 20:5, 18)

> "Behold, **tomorrow is the** chodesh, and I should not fail to sit with the king at meat."

David announces with certainty, the day before, that "tomorrow is the chodesh." This advance predictability fits the full moon (easily predicted by observing the waxing progression) rather than a crescent (weather-dependent, can be delayed 1-3 days).

### The Darkness Pattern

**Abraham's Covenant (Genesis 15:12, 17):** Sealed in "great darkness" â€” total darkness, no moonlight.

**The Exodus (Exodus 12:40-41; Numbers 33:3):** Departure on "the selfsame day," 430 years later, on the **15th day of the first month**.

For both events to occur on naturally dark nights at mid-month (15th), the month must begin at full moon and wane toward mid-month darkness.
