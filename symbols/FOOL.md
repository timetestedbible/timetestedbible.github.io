# FOOL — Word Study

## Symbolic Meaning

| Dimension | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **IS (One Word)** | Disobedient |
| **IS (Two Words)** | Self-Willed Rebel |
| **DOES (One Word)** | Rejects |
| **DOES (Two Words)** | Despises Instruction |
| **Sentence** | One who rejects God's instruction and follows their own heart/way — not lacking intelligence, but refusing to submit to God's wisdom and commandments |

**Opposite**: WISE = Obedient / One who does God's commandments

---

## The Key: The Opposite Defines the Meaning

Scripture repeatedly defines **WISDOM** as:

| Verse | Wisdom IS |
|-------|-----------|
| Psalm 111:10 | "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom... they that **DO his commandments**" |
| Proverbs 1:7 | "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge" |
| Proverbs 10:8 | "The wise in heart will **receive commandments**" |

**Therefore FOOL (the opposite) = One who does NOT do commandments = DISOBEDIENT**

---

## Primary Evidence

### 1. Direct Biblical Equation

> **Titus 3:3** — "For we ourselves also were sometimes **foolish, disobedient**, deceived"

Scripture directly pairs "foolish" and "disobedient" as synonyms.

### 2. Matthew 7:26 — The Explicit Definition

> "And every one that **heareth** these sayings of mine, and **doeth them NOT**, shall be likened unto a **foolish** man"

The FOOLISH man is explicitly defined as one who **hears but does NOT do** — this IS disobedience.

### 3. Galatians 3:1 — Foolish = Not Obeying

> "O **foolish** Galatians, who hath bewitched you, that ye should **not obey** the truth"

Paul equates foolishness with not obeying.

### 4. Psalm 14:1 / 53:1 — The Foundational Definition

> "The **fool** hath said in his heart, **There is no God**. They are corrupt, they have done abominable works, there is none that doeth good."

The fool denies God — and this denial manifests in corrupt works and not doing good. To deny God is to reject His authority, which is the root of disobedience.

## Parable Application

### Matthew 7:24-27 — The Two Builders

| Builder | Description | Symbolic Meaning |
|---------|-------------|------------------|
| **Wise** | "heareth... and **doeth them**" | OBEDIENT — acts on God's word |
| **Foolish** | "heareth... and **doeth them NOT**" | DISOBEDIENT — hears but rejects |

The wise built on ROCK (covenant), the foolish on SAND (broken covenant/unreliable).

### Matthew 25:1-13 — The Ten Virgins

| Virgins | Description | Symbolic Meaning |
|---------|-------------|------------------|
| **Wise** | Had OIL (proven works) | OBEDIENT — had built up works through obedience |
| **Foolish** | Had no OIL | DISOBEDIENT — had NOT built up works |

Both had LAMPS (outward appearance), but only the obedient had substance (proven works).

### Luke 12:16-21 — The Rich Fool

The rich man was called "fool" because he stored up treasure for SELF instead of being "rich toward God" — he followed his own way instead of God's priorities. This is disobedience in action.

## The 1 Corinthians 3:18 Paradox Resolved

> "If any man among you seemeth to be wise in this world, let him become a **fool**, that he may be wise."

This seeming contradiction resolves perfectly:

- **Wise in this world** = obedient to worldly wisdom/standards
- **Become a fool** = become "disobedient" to worldly wisdom
- **That he may be wise** = and thus become obedient to God's wisdom

To become "disobedient" to the world's false standard is to become truly obedient to God's true standard.

## Dual Application Test

| Subject | "Ye are the FOOL of the world" | Works? |
|---------|--------------------------------|--------|
| Negative | This describes people as disobedient to God's ways | ✓ Descriptive warning |
| 1 Cor 4:10 | "We are **fools** for Christ's sake" | ✓ Disobedient to world, obedient to Christ |

The dual sense works: Fool in the negative sense = disobedient to God. Fool in the paradoxical sense = disobedient to worldly wisdom (which means obedient to God).

## Summary

**FOOL** symbolically represents **DISOBEDIENCE** — one who hears God's instruction but refuses to do it, trusting instead in their own heart and following their own way.

This connects the symbolic web:
- **TRUTH** = God's Standard (what He commands)
- **FAITH** = Obedience (trusting response to that standard)
- **FOOL** = Disobedience (rejection of that standard)
- **WISE** = Obedience (doing that standard)

The fool is not primarily one who lacks intelligence, but one who lacks **submission** — the self-willed rebel who hears but does not do.
